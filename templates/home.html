The Main Parameters

The trend of precision agriculture is on the rise these days. Farmers are able to make informed decisions about their farming strategy with the help of this tool.

Ratio of Nitrogen content in soil

The ratio of nitrogen content in soil can vary depending on several factors, including the type of soil, the location, and the management practices used. In general, nitrogen is one of the most important nutrients for plant growth and is often a limiting factor for crop production.

Ratio of phosphorus content in soil

The ratio of phosphorus content in soil can vary widely depending on factors such as soil type, pH, and nutrient management practices. In general, phosphorus is present in soil in relatively small quantities compared to other macronutrients like nitrogen and potassium.

Ratio of potassium content in soil

The ratio of potassium content in soil can vary depending on factors such as soil type, pH, and nutrient management practices. Potassium is an important macronutrient for plant growth and is often present in soil in higher quantities than phosphorus.

Temperature in degree Celsius

The temperature in soil can vary depending on a variety of factors such as climate, depth, moisture content, and time of day or year. Generally, soil temperatures tend to lag behind air temperatures and may fluctuate less due to the insulating properties of the soil.

Relative humidity in %

Relative humidity is a measure of the amount of moisture in the air relative to the maximum amount of moisture that the air could hold at a given temperature. In soil, relative humidity refers to the amount of moisture in the soil relative to the maximum amount of moisture the soil could hold at a given temperature.

ph value of the soil

The pH value of soil is a measure of its acidity or alkalinity. It is measured on a scale from 0 to 14, where 7 is considered neutral, values below 7 are acidic, and values above 7 are alkaline.

Rainfall in mm

Rainfall is an important factor affecting soil moisture and fertility. The amount of rainfall required to maintain optimal soil moisture levels varies depending on factors such as soil type, vegetation cover, and temperature. In general, the amount of rainfall needed to maintain healthy soil moisture levels is between 500 to 800 millimeters (mm) per year.
